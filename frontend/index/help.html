Frequently Asked Questions

How can I calculate the impact of inflation on my savings?

To calculate the impact of inflation on your savings, you can use the following formula:

                Future Value of Savings = Current Value * (1 - Inflation Rate / 100)^Number of Years

For example, if you have ₹1,00,000 in savings and the inflation rate is 6% for 5 years, the value of your savings would be reduced to ₹74,000.

What should I know before investing in India’s stock market?
  • Research and Understanding: Learn about how the stock market works, types of investments, and risks involved.
  • Risk Tolerance: Assess how much risk you are willing to take.
  • Investment Horizon: Decide if your investment is short-term or long-term.
  • Diversification: Spread your investments across different sectors to reduce risk.
  • Regulatory Framework: Familiarize yourself with regulations from SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India).
How can I make sense of the relationship between GDP, inflation, and stock market?

The relationship between GDP, inflation, and the stock market is intertwined:

  • GDP Growth: A growing GDP generally indicates a strong economy, which tends to boost stock market performance.
  • Inflation: Moderate inflation can indicate healthy economic growth, but high inflation can reduce purchasing power and negatively impact stock prices.
  • Stock Market: Investors often react to GDP growth and inflation trends by adjusting their investments.

How often is the stock market updated and what time do markets open in India?

The Indian stock market is updated in real-time during market hours:

  • Market Hours: 9:15 AM IST to 3:30 PM IST, Monday to Friday.
  • Pre-opening session: 9:00 AM to 9:15 AM IST.
  • Post-market session: Updates on stock performance are available after market hours, but the official closing time is 3:30 PM IST.
